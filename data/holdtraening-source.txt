📁 Team Training
📁 Common (all positions) — 20

Scan at least twice before receiving the ball — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Look left shoulder → back to center → look right shoulder → back to center.
- Look for pressure (close) and free teammate (safe solution).
- Decide action before the ball reaches you.
Why it matters:
- You get "more time" on the ball and make faster decisions under pressure.

Identify pressure and free space — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Find nearest pressure (who can reach you in 1–2 sec?).
- Find a free space in which you can take the first touch.
- Choose a quick pass under close pressure.
Why it matters:
- You avoid panic touches and play more safely and forward.

Have a plan before the ball arrives — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Make plan A (forward) and plan B (safe).
- Check if plan A is still open just before receiving.
- Receive at an angle so you can execute the plan.
Why it matters:
- You play faster and more stable, especially in tight spaces.

First touch in the direction of play — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Receive in half turn with open body.
- First touch should move the ball 0.5–1 m in the direction you want to play.
- Avoid touching directly against pressure.
Why it matters:
- You create time, beat pressure and can play forward more often.

Avoid stopping the ball dead — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Never take the ball "dead" if there is pressure nearby.
- Use a small touch forward/to the side instead.
- Keep your body over the ball and be ready for the next action.
Why it matters:
- Stopping the ball dead invites pressure. Active first touch keeps you in control.

React quickly when possession is lost — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- React within 2 seconds: press the nearest or close the pass.
- Sprint the first 5 meters (not jogging).
- Communicate "pressure" so the team follows.
Why it matters:
- You stop transitions and win more balls high up the pitch.

Scan before receiving, even away from the ball — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Scan every 3-5 second, even when you don't expect the ball.
- Look for free teammate, pressure and next space.
- Increase scanning as the ball approaches your side.
Why it matters:
- You become faster in the head and calmer on the ball.

Play one touch when the situation allows it — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Scan before the ball arrives: find receiver.
- Stand at an angle so that the ball can be played on with one touch.
- Choose 1-touch especially for close pressure.
Why it matters:
- Tempo increases, pressure is broken, and you become harder to shut down.

Position yourself so you can play forward — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Stand "to the side" of the opponent, not behind him.
- Open your body towards the pitch.
- Move 1–2 m to create passing line forward.
Why it matters:
- You help the team play through chains and create chances.

Communicate before you receive the ball — ★★☆☆☆
How to:
- Give one clear message: "time", "man up", "turn", "one touch".
- Say it early (before receiving).
- Keep it short and repeat only when necessary.
Why it matters:
- Better decisions and fewer mistakes – especially at high speed.

Create a new angle after passing — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- After passing: move 2–5 m to new angle.
- Be available again (show hands/body towards ball).
- Look for the next space you can receive in.
Why it matters:
- You keep the game going and become a constant solution.

Be available under pressure — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Make small movements out of the "shadow".
- Stand at an angle so you can continue playing with 1–2 touches.
- Communicate "in feet" if you want the ball.
Why it matters:
- The team can play out of pressure, and you develop elite confidence on the ball.

Think about the next action, not the current one — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Think: "if You get it now, what will I do?"
- Always have a plan A/B.
- Receive with the body ready for the next action.
Why it matters:
- You become faster, more stable and make fewer panic errors.

Work actively in transitions — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- When winning the ball: make yourself available forward immediately.
- When losing the ball: press/close the center immediately.
- Focus on the first 5 meters.
Why it matters:
- Transitions create the most chances and the most dangerous situations.

Open body position before reception — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Stand at a 45° angle so you can see the pitch.
- Receive with a "half turn".
- Avoid standing completely flat against the ball.
Why it matters:
- You can play forward faster and avoid getting locked.

Change game side quickly when there is room — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Scan: where is the opposite side free?
- Play quickly via 1–2 passes or longer ball.
- Afterwards, move to a new support position.
Why it matters:
- You avoid pressure and create 1v1/2v1 on the opposite side.

Play through the pressure line when it is safe — ★★★★★
How to:
- Scan for fit "between chains".
- Use firm passing along the ground to free teammate.
- Choose safe solution if the line closes.
Why it matters:
- Breaking lines of pressure is a key to creating chances against good teams.

First 5 meters after delivery (re-position) — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- After passing: sprint/accelerate the first 5 m to a new place.
- See if you can get the ball back.
- Create superiority around the ball.
Why it matters:
- You are constantly involved and help the team dominate.

Keep the correct distance to the nearest teammate — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Too close = congestion, too far = no support.
- Adjust 2–3 m based on pressure.
- Keep "triangles" in the game.
Why it matters:
- Good spacing makes passing and repressing significantly better.

Choose a safe solution in your own dangerous zone — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- In own third: avoid risky passes centrally.
- Play out via sides/back if you are pressed.
- Only take risks when you have the time and support.
Why it matters:
- You reduce the most dangerous ball losses, which often lead to goals against.

How to:

- Choose quick, simple solution.
- Initiate while the opponent is off balance.
Why it matters:
- You create transitions and catch the opponent disorganized.

Select quick launch — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Plan A: fast forward. Plan B: safe map.
- Decide before you pick up the ball.
- Take it easy (no panic).
Why it matters:
- The pace and quality of your games will improve.

Actively position yourself in relation to the location of the ball — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Move in small steps as the ball moves.
- Center yourself in relation to the ball (angle-closure).
- Keep balance on the front foot.
Why it matters:
- You "save" with positioning and make the target smaller.

Be playable in the build-up — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Position yourself as an extra passing option.
- Receive at an angle to the next pass.
- Communicate to stop: “back”.
Why it matters:
- You can play out of pressure and keep the ball.

Make early decisions in the box — ★★★★★
How to:
- Read posts early: go/stay.
- If you go: go 100% and shout “MIN”.
- If you stay: sit down to shoot/return.
Why it matters:
- Reduces chaos and prevents cheap targets.

Control the tempo with the ball — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Fast when you are outnumbered.
- Slower when the team is going up.
- Use the same technique, only the decision changes.
Why it matters:
- You control the rhythm of the fight.

Communicate at set pieces — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Distribute zones and markings.
- Use clear words: "post", "out", "hold".
- Repeat just before post.
Why it matters:
- Dead balls decide matches – organization reduces dangerous finishes.

Anticipate the game – not just react — ★★★★★
How to:
- Read the ball carrier's body position.
- Move 1–2 steps before stabbing/insertion.
- Stay focused on dangerous spaces.
Why it matters:
- You become faster and safer in all situations.

Be ready for the return ball — ★★★★☆
How to:
- After rescue: country in balance.
- Expect new ending.
- Communicate “clear”/“min”.
Why it matters:
- Returns are a great source of goals. You close the "second chance".

Restore organization after rescue — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Shout "out", "up", "press".
- Collect the rear chain quickly.
- Create calm before you build up.
Why it matters:
- You avoid one rescue turning into 2–3 dangerous situations.

Position yourself as a sweeper-keeper at high pressure — ★★★★★
How to:
- Stand 5–15 m outside when standing tall.
- Be ready to clear stitches.
- Keep your body low and ready to sprint.
Why it matters:
- You can stand taller and push more aggressively without being penalized in the back.

Play out with both feet — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Receive and play with the freest foot.
- Be calm and keep a close eye on the ground.
- Avoid "locking" yourself to one side.
Why it matters:
- You become harder to press and the structure becomes stable.

Communicate at post (before the ball is hit) — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Say "post", "out", "hold line".
- Tell who is dueling and who is covering space.
- Repeat right before post.
Why it matters:
- Less confusion = less free headers against you.

Footwork before shot — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Small "set-steps" (quick small steps) before completion.
- Keep weight on front foot.
- Hands in front of body ready.
Why it matters:
- You become explosive and react faster.

Grip technique (safe balls) — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Meet the ball in front of the body.
- "W-hands" for high balls, bowl for lows.
- Lock the ball in to the chest.
Why it matters:
- Secure grip reduces returns and chaos.

Read playthroughs and stitches — ★★★★★
How to:
- Scan the depth of the back chain and the opponent's run.
- Anticipate stabbing when the ball holder has time.
- Choose early: go out/hold.
Why it matters:
- You stop the most dangerous chances: free runners and breakthroughs.

Distribution of roles for free kicks (wall + depth) — ★★★★☆
How to:
- Place the wall correctly (distance + number).
- Agree who covers depth/return.
- Check your position in relation to the post.
Why it matters:
- Reduces direct goals and rebounds after free kicks.

Trigger pressure with initiation — ★★★☆☆
How to:
- Start quickly to free side to avoid pressure.
- Use short passes to entice pressure and play through.
- Communicate “turn”/“again”.
Why it matters:
- You control where the preset comes and play freely.
